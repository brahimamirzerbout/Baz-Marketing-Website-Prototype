## The four chapters

### 01 · Audit — where the leaks are

The first 14 days of any engagement. Don't ship anything. Map the funnel. Find the leaks. Write the audit. Most founders skip this and start running paid. That's why most agencies fail.

**What to do:**
- Pull the last 90 days of paid, organic, and lifecycle metrics. All of them. One doc.
- Map the funnel step-by-step. Not "awareness → consideration → decision." Real: paid impression → site visit → MQL → SQL → closed-won.
- For each step, write the conversion rate, the volume, and the value.
- Find the step with the worst conversion at the highest volume. That's the biggest leak.
- Find the step with the worst value-per-touch. That's the most expensive waste.
- Run a senior review on the audit. Not the team. The senior.

**Exit criteria:** A one-page audit with 3 specific leaks, 3 specific fixes, and a forecast.

### 02 · Plan — the 90-day forecast

The 15-21 day phase. Take the audit and turn it into a plan. The plan is one document, not a deck. Decks are for selling. Plans are for shipping.

**What to do:**
- Write the 90-day plan in plain English. Five paragraphs: where we are, where we're going, what we're shipping, who's doing it, what gets measured.
- Pick 3 KPIs. Not 10. Three. The ones that map to revenue.
- Write the forecast. Be honest. If you can't hit 4× pipeline coverage, say so.
- Define exit criteria at 30/60/90. If we hit them, scale. If we don't, refund month 4.
- Sign the plan with the senior. Not the team. The senior.

**Exit criteria:** A signed plan with 3 KPIs, a written forecast, and an exit-criteria matrix.

### 03 · Ship — the weekly cadence

Days 22-75. The execution phase. This is where most agencies fail — they ship a big launch in week 1, then nothing for 6 weeks. BAZ ships every week.

**What to do:**
- Run a weekly sprint. Monday: plan. Tuesday-Thursday: ship. Friday: review.
- Every Friday, the senior reviews what's been shipped and what hasn't. The review is 30 minutes. No slides. The doc.
- Every two weeks, refresh the creative. For paid, that means 2-3 new hooks, 1-2 new formats, 1 test against the current winner.
- Every month, refresh the strategy. What's working, what isn't, what we're cutting, what we're adding.
- The senior writes the Friday review. Not the team. The senior.

**Exit criteria:** A weekly shipping log that an exec can scan in 2 minutes and know exactly what shipped, what didn't, and why.

### 04 · Measure — the exec dashboard

Days 30, 60, 90. The measurement phase. Run throughout, but the dashboard is the artifact that gets reviewed at 30/60/90 checkpoints.

**What to do:**
- Build one exec dashboard. Not five. One. With the 3 KPIs from the plan, plus the unit economics (CAC, LTV, MER, payback).
- Server-side tracking. Stop trusting Meta's "your campaign is crushing it" dashboard. They count every post-iOS signal as a paid conversion. You're not crushing it.
- Multi-touch attribution. Every touchpoint — paid, organic, email, sales, referral — recorded with a weight.
- Weekly exec read. The senior opens the dashboard Monday morning. The CMO opens it Tuesday. The board sees it monthly.

**Exit criteria:** A one-screen dashboard a CMO can open in under 60 seconds and know the state of the business.

## The loop in one diagram

```
[visitor] → [agent demo] → [score 0-100] → [route by intent]
                                          ↓
              [hot] → book_call · [warm] → send_proposal
              [cool] → 7d nurture · [cold] → 30d newsletter
              [tire_kicker] → archive
                                          ↓
                            [human reads the dashboard]
                                          ↓
              [senior calls the lead] → [closed-won or no]
```

This loop runs itself until a senior needs to talk to a human. The whole point of the BAZ Marketing Hub is to make sure that happens at the right moment, not the wrong one.
