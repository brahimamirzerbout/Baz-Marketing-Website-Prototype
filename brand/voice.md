# Voice — Copywriting, Tone & Messaging

BAZ writes like a magazine, not a brochure. Every word must earn its place.

---

## 1. Brand Voice

### Core Attributes

| Attribute | Description | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Confident** | We state what we believe, then prove it. | "We make brands unignorable." |
| **Specific** | Numbers over adjectives. | "240+ brands" not "many clients" |
| **Editorial** | Magazine-quality writing. | Short sentences. Period. |
| **Honest** | No bullshit. Constraints are stated. | "Senior team, no juniors." |
| **Opinionated** | The agency has a viewpoint. | "Brands are verbs, not nouns." |

### Anti-Voice (What We Never Do)

| Anti-Pattern | Example | Why |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Corporate jargon | "leveraging synergies" | Vacuous |
| Superlatives without proof | "world-class", "best-in-class" | Unsubstantiated |
| Exclamation marks | "We're so excited!!!" | Juvenile |
| Emoji in professional copy | "🚀 Let's go!" | Undermines authority |
| Passive voice | "Growth is delivered by BAZ" | Weak |
| Hedging language | "We might be able to help" | Lacks confidence |

## 2. Grammar & Style

### Rules

1. **Oxford comma** — "brands, growth, and revenue"
2. **Em dash** — Use `—` (not hyphen) for parenthetical statements
3. **Sentence case** for all headings — "Powering your path to growth" (not "Powering Your Path To Growth")
4. **Period** at the end of body copy, **no period** on headlines or CTAs
5. **One space** after periods, never two
6. **Numerals** for all numbers — "5 services" not "five services"
7. **No ampersands** in body copy — use "and" (except in logo: "Brand & Buzz")
8. **Abbreviations** — Spell out on first use, abbreviate after (SEO, CRM, CMO)

### Punctuation

| Mark | Rule | Example |
|------|------|---------|
| Period | Body copy only | "We make brands unignorable." |
| Em dash | Parentheticals | "Senior team — no juniors — means faster shipping." |
| No period | Headlines, CTAs | "Get Started" |
| Colon | Lists, labels | "Services:" |
| Bullet | List items | Use `●` for eyebrows, `—` for list items |

## 3. Headline Framework

BAZ headlines follow a consistent structure:

### Hero Headlines (Fraunces 300, 64–96px)
Short. Punchy. Period-optional.

- "Brand & Buzz."
- "We make brands unignorable."
- "From 0 to 100M views."

### Section Headlines (Fraunces 400, 36–48px)
Declarative. Specific. No questions.

- "240+ brands trust BAZ"
- "Senior team, no juniors"
- "Speed is a feature"

### Subheadings (Inter 400, 20px)
Supportive. Expanding. Never repeating the headline.

- Headline: "Brand & Buzz."
- Sub: "We make brands unignorable. From 0 to 100M views — we've done it."

## 4. Eyebrows

Every section starts with an eyebrow in JetBrains Mono, 11px, uppercase, tracking 0.18em, violet accent color, preceded by a `●` bullet.

```
● Brand & Identity
● Performance Marketing
● 240+ brands
● Case study
```

### Eyebrow Rules
- Always uppercase
- Always preceded by `●` with a space
- Maximum 3 words ideal, 5 words maximum
- Always in accent color (violet)
- Never a full sentence — it's a label, not a headline

## 5. CTA Copy

### Button Text Rules
- **Action-oriented** — Start with a verb
- **First person** — "Get my proposal" not "Get your proposal"
- **Specific** — "See our work" not "Learn more"
- **No generic text** — Never "Submit", "Click here", "Read more"

| Context | ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Primary CTA | "Start your project" | "Submit" |
| Secondary CTA | "See our work" | "Learn more" |
| Newsletter | "Get the briefing" | "Subscribe" |
| Contact | "Talk to us" | "Contact us" |
| Download | "Download the report" | "Download" |
| Pricing | "See pricing" | "Learn more" |

### CTA Arrow
Primary CTAs may include a trailing arrow: "Start your project →"

## 7. Email Copy

### Subject Lines
- 6–8 words maximum
- No ALL CAPS
- No emojis
- Specific > vague

| ✅ Good | ❌ Bad |
|---------|--------|
| "Your Q3 growth report" | "CHECK THIS OUT!!!" |
| "BAZ proposal for Acme" | "Following up" |
| "3 insights from our call" | "Quick question" |

### Email Body
- Short paragraphs (2–3 sentences max)
- One CTA per email
- Sign off: "— The BAZ team" (no name)

## 8. Social Media Copy

### Platform Tone Adjustments

| Platform | Tone | Length | CTA Style |
|----------|------|--------|-----------|
| **LinkedIn** | Professional, data-driven | 150–300 chars | "Read the full analysis →" |
| **X/Twitter** | Sharp, opinionated | 280 chars max | Link in bio |
| **Instagram** | Visual-first, editorial | 125 chars + hashtags | "Link in bio" |
| **YouTube** | Educational, authoritative | Title: 50 chars | "Watch next →" |

### Hashtag Rules
- Maximum 3 hashtags per post
- Always lowercase
- Always branded: #bazagency #brandandbuzz #unignorable
- Never: #blessed #grateful #hustle

## 9. Marketing Language

### The Five Brand Beliefs (Use as Section Eyebrows)

1. **Brands are verbs, not nouns.** — A brand is something a company *does*, not something it *has*.
2. **Distribution is design.** — The best creative in the world dies if nobody sees it.
3. **Senior team, no juniors.** — The people who pitch are the people who ship.
4. **Speed is a feature.** — Markets don't wait. We ship in weeks, not quarters.
5. **Measure what matters.** — Followers are vanity. Revenue is sanity.

### Power Phrases (Use in Marketing)

- "Powering your path to growth"
- "We make brands unignorable"
- "From 0 to 100M views — we've done it"
- "Senior team, no juniors, no bullshit"
- "Brands, buzz, and bottom-line growth"

### Prohibited Phrases

| ❌ Never | ✅ Instead |
|----------|-----------|
| "World-class" | "240+ brands served" |
| "Best-in-class" | "98% client retention" |
| "Cutting-edge" | "AI-powered workflows" |
| "Synergy" | "Growth" |
| "Holistic" | "End-to-end" |
| "Revolutionize" | "Transform" |
| "Disrupt" | "Change" |
