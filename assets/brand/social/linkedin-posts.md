## Post 1 — "Why we don't take clients under $2M ARR"

Most agencies will take anyone with a credit card.
We don't.

Here's why:

At $500K ARR, the math doesn't work. The senior team costs the same whether the client is at $500K or $5M. The revenue lift we can deliver is real, but it's not enough to cover the engagement.

At $2M ARR, the math starts to work. A senior who ships 4× pipeline coverage in 90 days creates $300K+ in net-new pipeline. That's a 6× return on a $50K engagement.

At $5M ARR and above, it's obvious. The senior is a no-brainer.

We turn down about 60% of the companies that reach out. Most of them aren't ready — they're looking for a band-aid, not a senior partner.

The clients we do take get 100% of the senior's attention. No junior pods. No handoffs. No "we'll get back to you with a strategy deck next week."

If that sounds like what you need, the door is open.

If it doesn't, save us both the call.

## Post 2 — "The agency churn equation"

Agencies churn at ~70% in year 1.

The math is simple: client signs retainer, agency promises the world, agency delivers 60% of the world, client fires agency at month 8, agency signs replacement at month 9, replacement delivers 50% of the world, client gives up on agencies entirely at month 18.

The pattern isn't bad agencies. It's the model.

Retainers incentivize hours, not outcomes. The agency gets paid whether the work moves revenue or not. The client can't tell the difference between "we tried hard" and "we shipped work that moved a number."

The fix is structural, not operational.

BAZ does it three ways:
1. **Written forecast at kickoff.** "We will hit 4× pipeline coverage by day 90. Here's the number."
2. **Quarterly renewals, not annual.** If we're not earning the engagement, you leave. No "we're locked in for 12 months."
3. **Refund month 4 if we miss.** No "that's the industry standard." No "we're working on it."

The result: zero churn in 2025. Every client who didn't hit their forecast refunded. Every client who hit, renewed.

This is the only agency model that aligns incentives. If you want to know how to evaluate one, ask: "What happens if you miss the forecast?"

If the answer is "we'll work harder," they're not aligned.

## Post 3 — "The hidden cost of iOS 14.5 on your attribution"

Two years after Apple's ATT prompt, most B2C brands are still running paid with broken attribution.

Here's what's happening:

1. User sees a Meta ad.
2. User clicks the ad.
3. Apple prompts the user: "Allow this app to track you?"
4. 70% of users say no.
5. The conversion happens — but Meta can't see it.
6. Meta's pixel fires a "view" event with a low weight.
7. Your dashboard counts it as a paid conversion.
8. You scale the campaign.
9. Your real CAC is 2.4× what the dashboard says.

The fix is server-side:
- Conversions API (CAPI) for Meta
- Enhanced Conversions for Google
- First-party cookies + a CDP (Segment, Rudderstack, or mParticle)
- A multi-touch attribution model that doesn't trust any single platform

We rebuilt attribution for a DTC brand last quarter. Their reported ROAS was 3.1×. After the rebuild, it was 1.8× — but their actual revenue was 2.3× higher than the dashboard claimed. They had been under-spending for 6 months.

If your CAC is "stable" and your LTV is "improving" and your dashboard says you're crushing it, but the bank account says otherwise, you have an attribution problem.

## Post 4 — "What a senior-only team actually does differently"

A junior team: plans, briefs, reviews, hands off.
A senior team: plans, ships, reviews, ships, reviews, ships.

The difference is in the shipping. A senior ships because they've shipped before. They know the pitfalls. They know the shortcuts. They know the things that look like wins but are actually wasted spend.

A junior team can run an A/B test. A senior team runs the A/B test, reads the result in 24 hours, and ships the winner before the junior team has written up the test plan for the next one.

A junior team can write an SEO brief. A senior team writes the brief, edits it to match what the senior writer is going to ship, and reviews the first draft before the writer has finished the second.

Speed compounds. The senior's cycle is 3-5× faster than the junior's. Over 90 days, the senior ships 3-5× the work. Over a year, the senior ships 10× the work.

That's why we don't have juniors.
